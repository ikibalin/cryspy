# $$\lambda/2$$ contamination


For experiments with single crystal the correction of $$\lambda/2$$ contamination is
introduced by a nonzero coefficient $$c$$ in front of unpolarized integrated intensity with doubled Miller indices $$I_{2h2k2l}$$:

$$ I_{2h2k2l} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \left( I^{UP}_{2h2k2l} + I^{DOWN}_{2h2k2l} \right) $$

$$ FR = \frac{I^{UP}_{hkl} + c \cdot I_{2h2k2l}}{I^{DOWN}_{hkl} + c \cdot I_{2h2k2l}} $$

In the calculations of the extinction correction $$y$$ for $$(2h2k2l)$$ reflections the half of the wavelength is used.
